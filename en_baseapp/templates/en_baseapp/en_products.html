Vitamin-Herbal Flour (VHF) from alfalfa in the form of granules is a wonderful product, both in pure form and as part of grain feed.

Granulated Vitamin-Herbal Flour (GVHF) is a vitamin-protein feed got from dried and granulated herbs of perennial plants (alfalfa). In the process of granulation, hay undergoes heat treatment at a temperature of 150-250 degrees, at such temperatures all pathogenic bacteria die in the hay. In terms of nutritional value, GVHF is close to traditional grain feeds, and in terms of the content of biologically active substances it surpasses them. Using granulated grass flour in animal diets has a beneficial effect on the growth and development of young animals, normalizes digestion, provides the animal body with vitamins, amino acids, macro-microelements, and helps to increase productivity and safety. It is especially important to use GVHF in winter.

Straw granules

Straw granules for bedding in Horse stalls. What are the advantages of straw granules and why are they inferior to the usual litter for stalls according to a number of criteria? Unlike sawdust, straw granules absorb twice as much moisture and perfectly retain the smell of ammonia, and waste from such a litter is an excellent fertilizer, unlike extremely slowly decomposing sawdust. In addition, the latter may contain chemicals that were used to treat trees, which will definitely not have a positive effect on the health of the horse.

Straw granules undergo heat treatment, which helps to get rid of spores and mold, which are highly susceptible to ordinary straw used as a litter. This material, despite its low price and ease of processing, loses to straw granules with an even more devastating score than sawdust. Due to the high absorbency of straw granules, their consumption during the year is much less than straw, which, moreover, is more difficult to remove from the stall.

The environmentally friendly granules produced by our company, which do not emit harmful phenols, are made of wheat straw and have a diameter of 8 mm. They need to be evenly distributed over the stall until a layer of 65 mm high is formed, and after a few minutes you can let the horse in here. Under the weight of its weight, the granules immediately begin to grind, and after a couple of days they will turn into a homogeneous soft litter.

Pellets are an excellent option for equestrian clubs with contact zoos or private stables, where, in addition to horses, there are other pets: rabbits, sheep and others, because straw pellets are so versatile that they are suitable, of course, not only for horses.

The daily care of such a litter is simple: the areas with granules that need to be removed are clearly visible. As necessary (preferably once a week), fresh straw granules should be added, and the litter should be completely replaced once a year. The waste will become a high-quality fertilizer.
